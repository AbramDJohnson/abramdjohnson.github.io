Bio

Abram Johnson is a second-year student at Howard Community College. He is currently majoring in Audio Video Media Production. During his time in the AVMP program he has learned about the different elements that make up a movie and has had the opportunity to individually practice them in various ways. He has edited a short documentary along with a musical montage and a short 17-minute film. In addition to that, Abram has also worked in music/ambience creation and has done sound design for two short animations along with two BIFSC animated films. Lastly, he has developed and visualized a hypothetical film concept and most of its aspects. His passion for filmmaking stems from childhood, wherein he enjoyed single-handedly animating, sound designing, and editing short stop motion videos as a hobby. When he's not busy with life or school responsibilities, he enjoys learning how to play guitar, cooking, reading, exploring film history, and writing screenplays. Upon completion of the AVMP program at HCC, he plans to transfer to four-year college and pursue a degree in Film and Television
